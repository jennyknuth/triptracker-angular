FAQ

How are awards calculated?

Participants earn one trip tracker dollar for every four, one-way trips (25 cents per trip). They earn another dollar for every ten miles of walking, biking, skating or scooting (active transportation). There is a two dollar bonus for students who complete every possible trip (max trips) of the month using alternatives to family car transport to school.

What if my child regularly goes to school from two different locations during the month?

Trip Tracker would like to have trips reported separately from the two locations because the mileage and possibly the mode of transportation will be different for each location. We can then combine the awards in one package for distribution. If only one household wishes to register in Trip Tracker, the reporting parent should report trips from both locations and put modifying information in the open ended comment section.

What if my child walks to the bus? How can they get credit for that as active transportation?

The Trip Tracker calendar will specifically ask you for that information.

We only live one block away from school. How far does my child need to walk each day to get mileage credit?

The minimum distance that Trip Tracker credits is .25 miles, the equivalent of at least two city blocks (although ‘blocks’ vary greatly in size). If you live closer then .25 miles, your child still gets trip credit, i.e. 4 trips = 1 trip tracker dollar.

Do participating businesses accept Trip Tracker Dollars from adults? Can I buy them from my daughter and then use them myself?

It’s up to the business but we don’t have any problem with big people using Trip Tracker Dollars. Now that we're also rewarding BVSD staff members for not driving to work, adults spending Trip Tracker dollars has become pretty normal.

Can I trade in last year's 'expired' dollars for this year's dollars?

No, we can’t afford it. We need to 'close the books' at the end of the year knowing that there are no more dollars in the system to be spent. We reimburse the businesses at 50 cents on the dollar for every dollar that they collect and fund raise the money to do that. If we kept money in the system, we wouldn't know how much money we needed to raise each year for reimbursement.

What if my child is out sick or has a meeting that they need to go to when you’re handing out the dollars at school? How do they get them?

Some schools will hold the left-over dollars in the office and some prefer not to do that. You can always send your request in a stamped, self-addressed envelope to Peter Hurst, BVSD Transportation, 6500 Arapahoe Rd., Boulder 80303.

Do I really have to keep track of this? Can’t my kids do it instead?

Sure, have your children be responsible for keeping track of their trips. Since this is a self-reporting system, we would like you to take a look at their record keeping to keep it honest and accurate. If you don’t want to relate to the monthly data collections survey, you can just sign the calendar from and have them bring it to your school’s main office at the end of the month.

I drop them off at school on my way to work. Is there some way they can still participate?

Yes and here is how you do it. Every trip that your child makes to or from school that involves at least .25 miles of walking, can be counted. So, if you drive them, you can let them out a few blocks away from school and let them walk. Have them meet a friend there and they can take advantage of the opportunity to 'walk and talk'.

How do you define a carpool? Does taking my three children to school count as a carpool?

We define carpools as multi-family. If you need any help with finding a carpool match, please contact us so we may be of assistance. Another way to allow your single family carpool to participate is to let them out at least a few blocks from school and have them register as walkers.

What’s to keep kids from adding trips they didn't make to their schedules or parents from giving inflated numbers so their children can ‘win’?

Our intent is to have Trip Tracker be as fair as possible within the limitations of the situation. Our experience is that parents want to model honesty for their children and will encourage them to only record actual trips. For kids who use the paper calendar system instead of the online survey, we require that it be signed by parents. Finally, we attempt to publish the names of monthly winners in the school newsletters and post them at school so that we can both acknowledge winners and give contestants the opportunity to point out blatant falsification of data.
